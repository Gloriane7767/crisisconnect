The Challenge

Three gaps blocking aid delivery

The Cameroon crisis is one of the world's most neglected displacement emergencies. IDPs face critical barriers in accessing the help they need.

📡

Information Gap

IDPs don't know what aid is available, where it is, or how to access it — especially in their own language.

🔗

Coordination Gap

NGOs and agencies lack a unified system to match available resources to specific IDP needs in real time.

🌐

Language Gap

Aid communication happens mostly in French, but IDPs speak Pidgin English, Fulfulde, and other local languages.

How It Works

Prompt engineering at the core

CrisisConnect uses carefully designed system prompts to guide Claude AI into acting as a humanitarian specialist.

1

IDP sends a message

Via WhatsApp, SMS, or this web interface — in any language they speak.

2

AI identifies needs

The system prompt guides Claude to ask about location, need type, and vulnerabilities.

3

Matches to resources

Claude recommends the nearest verified organization — IOM, UNHCR, WFP, MSF, UNICEF, and others.

4

Actionable next step

Each response ends with a clear follow-up so the IDP always knows what to do next.

Ethical Considerations

Responsible AI for vulnerable people

Every AI tool that serves displaced people carries serious responsibilities. Here is how CrisisConnect addresses them.

Security Risk

Location data could endanger IDPs

Armed groups could exploit location data shared with the chatbot.

No GPS or exact addresses collected. General zones only.

Bias Risk

AI trained mostly on English/French

Fulfulde and Pidgin speakers may get lower quality responses.

Prompts tested in local languages. Community validators used.

Misinformation Risk

Wrong aid location = dangerous journey

Outdated aid point info could send IDPs to locations with no help.

Aid data verified weekly. Users reminded to confirm before traveling.

Exclusion Risk

Many IDPs have no phone

A web/WhatsApp-only tool excludes the most marginalized.

USSD and community radio channels planned for offline access.

Dependency Risk

AI should not replace aid workers

Over-reliance on AI reduces human accountability in crisis response.

Positioned as a complement. Human override always available.

Transparency Risk

Users may not know it's AI

Deceptive AI in humanitarian contexts erodes trust.

AI identity disclosed at every conversation start.
